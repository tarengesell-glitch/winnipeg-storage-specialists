On-Site Protocol

Your home, treated like the asset it is.

This is not a noisy demolition crew tracking through your house. Every project follows our site protocol — a sequenced, contained, professional process that protects the rest of your home while we transform one room.

  1. Floor & surface protection

    Adjacent floors, walls, and finishes are masked and protected before the first tool touches the site.

  2. Contained dust zones

    Plastic barriers, zipper doors, and HEPA-grade air scrubbers keep demolition dust isolated to the work area.

  3. Daily cleanup

    The site is left tidy at the end of every day. No piles of debris growing in the corner of your home.

  4. Sequenced phasing

    Loud, dusty, and disruptive work is sequenced into the shortest possible window — so daily life keeps moving.

  5. Total debris removal

    Every cabinet, every offcut, every bag of dust — hauled away by us. We leave nothing behind for you to deal with.

  6. Single point of contact

    One project manager who answers your call, runs every trade, and owns the schedule from day one to walk-through.

Quietly professional. Visibly different.

A renovation should not turn the rest of your home into a construction site. If you've ever watched dust travel two floors away from a kitchen demo, you know exactly what we mean.

Our crews are trained to a higher site standard because our clients expect a higher site standard. Boots come off. Tools get cleaned. Drop sheets stay laid. The home is treated as the home — not as a worksite that happens to have furniture in it.

Common Questions

Everything you wanted to ask before calling.

Why are you so much more expensive than the cabinet store down the road?
Because we are not selling cabinets. We are redesigning the room itself, custom-building millwork to fit your life, handling engineering, permits, inspections, every trade, debris removal, and finish work — all under one firm. A cabinet store sells you a kit and an installer. We deliver a transformed room, end to end. The dollar amounts aren't comparable because the projects aren't comparable.
Do I really need a permit for a pantry or closet?
If walls move, framing changes, mechanical relocates, or load-bearing structure is touched — yes, you need a permit, an engineered drawing, and a City of Winnipeg inspection. Anything less is a future problem. Winnipeg is increasingly enforcing tear-outs of unpermitted work. We never put our clients in that position.
What happens if a contractor skipped the permit on previous work?
It can become an expensive problem at any time — often during a future renovation, a sale, or an insurance claim. The city can require unpermitted work to be opened, re-engineered, and rebuilt. We can usually identify these issues during the initial assessment and quote a remediation alongside the new design.
How long does a custom transformation take?
Design typically takes 3–6 weeks. Permitting adds 2–4 weeks. The on-site build is usually 2–6 weeks depending on scope. You receive a firm schedule before construction begins, and our project manager updates you weekly.
How disruptive is the work to daily life?
Far less than a typical renovation. We protect adjacent surfaces, contain dust zones with HEPA-grade air scrubbers, sequence loud and dusty work into the tightest windows, clean daily, and haul every bit of debris off-site. Most clients tell us they were surprised at how normal the rest of the house remained.
Do you handle every trade or do I need to hire others?
We handle every trade. Designer, engineer, framer, electrician, plumber, drywaller, painter, flooring, finish carpenter, millworker — all coordinated under one project manager. You speak to one person. You sign one contract. You receive one warranty.
What if I only want to transform one space — is that worth it?
Absolutely. Most clients start with one room and add more later. A single transformed pantry or closet often changes how the entire main floor functions — and how the home feels every morning.
Do you offer a warranty?
Yes — written workmanship warranty from us, plus the manufacturer warranty on hardware and fixtures. If anything is not right, we come back.
